What to do in an Accident
  • Safety First: Pull over to a safe area, turn on hazard lights, and check for injuries. Call 911 immediately if emergency care is required.
  • Swap Information: Share your name, contact phone, and this digital Insurance policy with other drivers involved.
  • Document Scene: Take photos of all vehicle damages, license plates, the accident intersection, and the other driver's license card.
  • File a Police Report: In many jurisdictions, calling the police is required to create a formal collision record for insurance.

Welcome to Vehicle Vault

Application guide & offline privacy disclosure

Vehicle Vault is your personal offline assistant designed to track car maintenance schedules, log service history, analyze insurance premiums, and aid you during roadside incidents.

100% Private & Offline Storage

Your privacy is our priority. No data, vehicle profiles, logs, or card uploads are ever sent to Thorpe Tech servers. All information is compressed and stored locally in your browser's local storage.

⚠️ CRITICAL BACKUP REQUIREMENT: Clearing your browser cookies or cache will erase all your profiles. Please use the Save Backup or Google Drive Save buttons in the header toolbar regularly to avoid data loss.

What the App Does

🔧 Maintenance Schedule Log oil changes, rotations, or air filters. Set up custom mileage and date notifications.
📊 Prorated Expense Charts Prerate insurance rates (Monthly, Semi-Annual, Annual) and compare spending in grouped bars.
📂 Document Scan Glovebox Scan card photos (License, Reg, Policy) and downscale sizes automatically to fit browser quotas.
🚨 Roadside Accident Hub Instantly call emergency services or carriers, follow crash safety guides, and log details.

How to Get Started

  1. Add your vehicle make, model, current mileage, and premium rates via + Add Vehicle.
  2. Open the vehicle card, click Digital Glovebox, and scan documents to prepare for roadside emergencies.
  3. Update mileage or log services whenever you visit a repair shop. Add receipts to logs.
  4. Regularly export database files as backup to prevent data loss.
